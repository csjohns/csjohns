About me

I am a PhD candidate in Political Science at the University of Washington in Seattle, WA, and a consultant at the Center for Social Science Computation and Research

I am working on a dissertation evaluating community-level democratic outcomes from the implementation of participatory budgeting, in which citizens of a community are given control over some part of the government's budget. I have taken field exams in comparative politics, political communication, and social science methodology. I have graduate certification in social science statistics, but have maintained a focus on fieldwork and ethnographic methods, and use multiple methods with great enthusiasm in my research. I am affiliated with the Center for Statistics and the Social Sciences and the Center for Comparative and Historical Analysis of Organizations and States

Prior to my return home to the lovely Pacific Northwest, I received my A.B. from Harvard with a joint concentration in Social Studies and Gender Studies, worked enthusiastically in Green Party organizing in Massachusetts, ran for State Representative, and realized I wanted to learn more about politics more formally. This took me to Oxford University for an MPhil in Politics (Comparative Government), after which I tried working outside of academia as a research assistant with the London-based global governance and accountability thinktank the One World Trust and spending a while absorbing the public sector culture in England. I realized two things: 1) I wanted to pursue a PhD and 2) I really wanted to know what was going on with the wave of "public involvement" promotion I was participating in in London. This brought me back to Seattle and the excellent training at the University of Washington.

Outside academia, I'm an active climber and mountaineer. When I feel like I have too much time on my hands (hah!)I occasionally will do a theatrical lighting design.
